## About me

PhD researcher in Learning Sciences and Digital Technologies, working at the intersection of **environmental sociology**, **AI literacy**, and **civic education**. My research investigates how young people develop ecological and digital competencies in the age of artificial intelligence.

Laurea in Sociologia (Università degli Studi di Salerno, 2004). Previously: sociologist in nonprofit and public sector contexts; university lecturer in Sociologia Generale and Sociologia dell'Educazione.

## Research Projects

### EcoAI Framework
An interdisciplinary theoretical and empirical framework integrating **Ecoliteracy** and **AI Literacy** into civic education. Four domains: Eco-Digital Information Literacy · AI Critical Attitudes · Eco-Civic Values · Eco-Digital Agency.

[www.ecoaiframework.com](https://www.ecoaiframework.com)

---

### EcoAI Index
A psychometric instrument validated via Confirmatory Factor Analysis (CFA) and Structural Equation Modelling (SEM) on 601 upper-secondary school students in southern Italy.  
Five latent dimensions · CFI=0.978 · RMSEA=0.063

### Eco-Digital Posthumanism
Theoretical framework proposing **Eco-Digital Co-Responsible Agency** as an emergent form of posthuman subjectivity at the intersection of ecological and digital systems.

[Read the preprint — SocArXiv](https://doi.org/10.31235/osf.io/u4mrn_v1)

---

### The Algorithmic Oracle *(under review)*
A sociology of delegated judgment under uncertainty. The paper argues that LLMs are being collectively instituted as oracular devices — not through institutional ritual mediation, but through privately enacted, infrastructurally governed delegation of interpretive authority. Drawing on Castoriadis's theory of imaginary institution, Stiegler's mimetic technology, and the comparative anthropology of divination, the paper proposes the concept of **algorithmic oracle** as a relational configuration and advances a falsifiable research agenda.
